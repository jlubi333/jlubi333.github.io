About Me

TL;DR: I am a programmer, pianist, and fencer.

Hello, I'm Justin, if you can't tell. I'm fifteen and I love to code in HTML, CSS, Javascript, Java, C++, and Python. I also try (read: hit random letters) to code x84 Assembly. My first programming experience was when I was 11. I dabbled in some Javascript—before I even knew HTML! After about a month of learning all about ifs and fors, I decided to learn HTML and CSS. I then looked towards Java, but it was way over my head. After about a year (I can't remember exactly how long), I buckled down and learned Java from thenewboston. Once I knew Java, nothing was stopping me from learning more programming languages! I went into iPhone development and learned Objective-C, then I went back to more advanced Java and some game development. Currently one of my favorite things to do is Project Euler, although whether I'm good at it remains to be seen. I view it as math problems with a side of computer science, as opposed to the other way around, and, although I love math, I am far stronger in pure CS.

In ninth grade, I took Computer Science AP, a sophomore course. The language of choice for the AP course is Java. The introductory course was in Python, but it was aimed towards complete beginners. I figured I ought to learn Python, too, and I'm very glad I did. Compared to languages like Java, it is incredibly easy to use! I learned a lot of Python over a weekend.

I also love to play the piano. I've been playing for about two and a half years, and I'm really happy with my progress. I tried violin out for a while, but I have a bit much on my plate now, so I may continue that another day—way in the future.

Speaking of too much on my plate, I'm in the robotics club, the fencing team, the Spanish team, and, on top of my academics, it can be a lot. I had never fenced before I got to my high school, and I love it! I also won first place in my division at the Florida State Spanish Conference. I was shocked.
